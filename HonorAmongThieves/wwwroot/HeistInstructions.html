INTRODUCTION

You are professional thief. Or at least, you're trying to be one.
You wouldn't be one if the job market wasn't this terrible.
Fortunately, your parents have granted you a sizable fortune to live off.
This fortune also paid for your 2 masters degrees in social work and fine art. Which might explain your job situation.

OBJECTIVE

The objective of this game is to be the richest thief by the time you retire.
The range of years the game lasts is set by the room creator.
The actual number of years the game lasts is a random number within that range.

HEIST OCCURRENCE

Every year, you will take part in a heist.
For everyone's security, your heist mates are chosen by an arbitrary third party.
Each heist consists of 2 or more thieves (including you).
The reward for a heist increases the larger it is (i.e. more thieves equals more money per person).
In the lobby of every heist, you can see the amount of years each fellow thief has spent in jail, and their approximate networth.

GOING ON HEISTS

You will be given a choice of whether or not to take part in the heist.
If half (rounded up) or more of the thieves in the heist choose to take part, the heist will occur.
The total reward is always the same. So the fewer thieves who take part, the larger the reward per thief.
If more than half of the thieves don't show up, the heist does not happen.
Regardless of whether the heist happens, all the thiefs that opt to take part will know of each other.
Note that you can uncheck the box to not go on the heist.

SNITCHING

You can also opt to snitch on the thieves in the same heist as you.
If you snitch on them, and the heist happens, then every single thief that went on the heist goes to jail, and you get reward money.
Snitching on a heist also means that the heist does not dish out any reward.
If you snitch on them, and the heist doesn't happen, you get put in jail and fined.
If multiple thieves snitch, the reward money is split between them.
Note that you can snitch on a heist you take part in.
Also note if you snitch on a heist, even if you don't go on it, you can still be blackmailed!
Lastly, note that you can uncheck every box and go on no heists, nor snitch, that year.

GOING TO JAIL

Your jail sentence is a random number between your minimum and maximum jail terms.
Going to jail will always increase your next jail sentence.
Going to jail also makes you lose a portion of your money.
Unless you went to jail because you reported on your own crime, in which case you don't lose money. And are only sentenced the minimum amount.

COMMITTING BLACKMAIL

You can also opt to blackmail a thief in the same heist as you.
This option makes you go on the heist.
If the other thief does not show up for the heist and does not snitch, then you can't blackmail him.
In the event that the other thief has snitched on an earlier heist, or is snitching on the current heist, you blackmail him.
A thief that is blackmailed, if he chooses to snitch, will not successfully snitch.
Successfully blackmailing another thief will give you a large portion of his fortune.
However, if you try to blackmail a thief who has never snitched on a heist that occurred, you get thrown into jail.
If you're already in jail, your jail term will be extended.
Note that if the Snitch Blackmail Window is set to -1, that means that the window is permanent.
If multiple players successfully black a thief at the same time, the blackmail reward is split evenly.

SPECIAL CONDITIONS

Blackmailing happens before snitching - so if you blackmail someone who is snitching on that current round, that snitch fails.
If multiple people blackmail each other, blackmail money tranfers happen simultaneously.
Even if you have nothing to do, you have to click the 'OKAY' button for events to continue.
